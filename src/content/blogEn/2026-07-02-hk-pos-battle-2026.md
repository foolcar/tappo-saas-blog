## First Things First: There's No "Best" POS — Only the Right One For You

A lot of restaurant owners start with: "Which POS is the best?" That's the wrong question.

Hong Kong's food scene is incredibly fragmented — the pain points of a Sham Shui Po cha chaan teng (tea restaurant) have almost nothing in common with those of a Central fine dining establishment. The first needs stable checkout and fast ticket printing; the second needs membership management, reservation systems, wine inventory, and multi-location data synchronization.

This article isn't going to tell you which one is "best." It's here to help you understand what you actually need in about 5 minutes, then see which system makes the most sense for you.

### 1. Eats365 — The Modular Benchmark

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Founded | 2014, Hong Kong-based |
| Pricing | Monthly subscription + modular add-ons |
| Entry Price | HK$188/month (annual basic plan) |
| OS | iPad (iOS) |
| Website | eats365pos.com |

Eats365 feels like a system you won't regret expanding into — it's built to grow with you.

**Strengths ✅**

- **Best delivery platform integration**: Currently the only provider in Hong Kong that publicly claims free native integration with all three major platforms — Deliveroo, Foodpanda, and Keeta. All orders flow into one backend, no app-switching required. For delivery-heavy restaurants, this alone saves real labor costs.

- **Excellent modular architecture**: 13+ functional modules, restaurants pick what they need. A new small shop can start with basic POS and add KDS (Kitchen Display System), CRM, or self-ordering kiosks later. This matters — many Hong Kong restaurants grow from small beginnings. Locking them into a full suite from day one means paying for modules they won't use for months.

- **Open integration ecosystem**: 100+ API integrations including Eber, JuicySuite (CRM), Xero (accounting), and multiple reservation platforms. Restaurants aren't locked into a single vendor — switching CRM or payment providers doesn't require switching the entire POS.

- **TVP (Technology Voucher) support**: Has helped multiple Hong Kong restaurants successfully apply for government funding. A real plus for owners planning digital transformation with subsidies.

- **Strong brand case studies**: Real Hong Kong restaurants like Loong Dim Sum (multiple locations) use it.

**Weaknesses ❌**

- **iPad hardware cost**: Running primarily on iPad means decent upfront investment — HK$5,000-8,000 for a complete setup (iPad + stand + printer) if starting from zero.

- **Feature overload for small shops**: For a tiny 3-table operation, Eats365's richness adds unnecessary complexity.

- **Advanced tier pricing**: The HK$488/month advanced plan, plus hardware installments and consumables, can easily push monthly costs past HK$1,000 — a meaningful percentage for a small shop doing HK$100K-200K monthly revenue.

### 2. iCHEF — The iPad POS Veteran from Taiwan

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|

| Pricing | Monthly subscription |
| Monthly Fee | HK$788-888/month (1 iPad, basic support included) |
| OS | iPad (iOS) |
| Website | www.ichefpos.com |

Honestly, iCHEF has the best-looking interface I've ever used in a POS system. But in Hong Kong, good looks alone won't cut it.

**Strengths ✅**

- **Industry-leading UI/UX**: iCHEF's design is arguably the most thoughtful in the POS space. Button sizes, color coding, and workflow logic have been refined through extensive testing in Asian restaurants. Staff onboarding is noticeably faster than competitors — a real advantage in an industry with high turnover.

- **Deep understanding of Asian dining**: Taiwan and Hong Kong share many dining patterns — hawker stalls, fast-paced service, complex customization ("less sweet, no ice, extra noodles"). iCHEF handles these better than Western brands.

- **Local Hong Kong team**: Cantonese-speaking support and local engineers on the ground. No waiting on English email support when something breaks at 10 PM.

- **Delivery integration improving**: Supports foodpanda and Uber Eats integration — adequate for most restaurants, though not as comprehensive as Eats365's three-platform coverage.

**Weaknesses ❌**

- **Premium pricing**: HK$788-888/month puts iCHEF at the higher end of the Hong Kong market. With iPad hardware, year-one total cost approaches HK$15,000 — a meaningful barrier for budget-conscious owners.

- **Food-only, no retail**: If you run a hybrid operation (coffee shop selling beans, bakery selling packaged goods), you'll need a separate retail POS, creating a management gap.

- **Localization gaps**: Despite Traditional Chinese support, some uniquely Hong Kong scenarios (dim sum cart tracking, barbecue meat pricing by weight, cha chaan teng combo customization) aren't as flexible as local brands.

- **Limited scalability**: Compared to Eats365's 100+ API ecosystem, iCHEF's third-party integration options are narrower.

- **Unmatched Chinese restaurant adaptability**: Dim sum cart tracking, seafood market pricing, roast meat by-weight billing, combo substitutions — Gingersoft handles these complex Chinese dining scenarios maturely. The interface may not win design awards, but for older floor staff, its familiarity is an asset.

**Weaknesses ❌**

- **Slow on modern features**: QR code self-ordering, automatic delivery platform integration, cloud-based multi-store sync — features that are standard on Eats365 often require custom development or version upgrades on Gingersoft.

- **Keeta support unclear**: Keeta (Meituan's Hong Kong delivery arm) has been rapidly gaining market share, but Gingersoft's Keeta integration status should be confirmed before signing.

### 7. Tappo — Pay-Per-Order, Zero Monthly Fee, Open Platform

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Positioning | On-demand restaurant operations platform |
| Pricing | Credit-based (1 order = 1 credit, no monthly fee, no contract) |
| Pricing Reference | 1,000 credits for HK$49.90, new users get 500 free credits (30-day validity) |
| OS | iPad / Android tablet |
| Website | gotappo.com |

In a market where every competitor says "most features" or "most local," Tappo takes a different approach. It doesn't define itself as a "POS software" — it's an **open restaurant operations platform** whose core proposition is: remove the monthly subscription lock-in, let restaurants pay by actual usage, and connect delivery, marketing, and chain management through an ecosystem.

> ⚠️ **Disclosure**: I work on the Tappo team. I'll do my best to be objective below, but please read with appropriate skepticism.

**Strengths ✅**

- **Zero monthly fee, pay-per-use model**: Virtually every POS system in Hong Kong is subscription-based (HK$188-888/month, plus hardware). Tappo uses a "1 order = 1 credit" model — no monthly fees, no long-term contracts. For shops doing fewer than 100 orders per day, total cost can be 50%+ lower than traditional subscription models. New users get 500 free credits (30-day validity) — enough to fully test all features.

- **Ecosystem connectivity**: Tappo's most distinct differentiator vs. local competitors. Through the **MOS ecosystem platform**, it connects with services like **Minitable** (Hong Kong's leading reservation system), **Cando** (the technology backbone for Greater Bay Area multi-location restaurant SaaS), and delivery platforms (Deliveroo, Foodpanda). Unlike competitors' closed all-in-one packages, Tappo follows an "app ecosystem" logic — restaurants pick the third-party tools that fit their needs.

- **Three-layer architecture**: The platform is structured into three capability modules:
  - **MOS (Cloud Brain)**: Ecosystem aggregation + data hub — connects delivery platforms, chain management, and digital marketing channels
  - **Station (Operations Terminal)**: In-store ordering, table management, kitchen printing, flexible payments, chain control — works offline
  - **Pulse (Customer Reach)**: QR ordering, membership points, coupons — real-time data feeds back into MOS

- **No hardware lock-in**: Use your own iPad or Android tablet. For owners with spare devices, upfront cost is essentially zero.

- **Extremely transparent pricing**: Website clearly lists credit packages and per-credit cost (HK$0.040-0.0499), plus a cost calculator. Among all compared brands, this is one of the best in pricing transparency.

- **Fast setup (under 30 minutes)**: From download to first order, the official claim is as fast as 10 minutes. For restaurants unsure about committing, the onboarding cost is minimal.

**Weaknesses ❌**

- **Feature depth behind market leaders**: Honestly, while core features are solid, Tappo still lags behind mature products like Eats365 and iCHEF in advanced features — KDS (Kitchen Display System), self-ordering kiosks, advanced CRM — all currently on the roadmap. If you need these now, Tappo isn't the right choice.

- **Brand too new, insufficient case studies**: Tappo is young. Installation count is far behind Gingersoft's 1,200+ or Eats365's thousands. For tradition-minded owners who value "stability" and "word of mouth," this is a trust gap that only time and real case studies can close.

- **Ecosystem still expanding**: The number and depth of ecosystem partners is growing. Before relying on a specific integration, confirm its status.

- **Enterprise features in development**: Advanced permissions, multi-location management, and BI analytics are still on the roadmap. This is the honest current state, and the team is actively closing the gap.

- **Per-order cost diminishes at high volume**: The model is very competitive for low-order-volume shops. But at 1,000+ orders/day, the total cost can exceed fixed monthly subscriptions — use the online calculator to compare.

- **Very low market awareness**: In Hong Kong's mainstream restaurant circles, Tappo's name recognition is minimal. It currently attracts new shop owners open to trying fresh solutions.
